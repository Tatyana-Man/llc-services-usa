How to Choose the Best LLC Service

The best LLC service depends on your business model, state, budget, and how much support you want after formation. A cheap formation package may be enough for a simple side project, while a growing company may benefit from stronger compliance tools and registered agent support.

1. Look beyond the advertised formation price

Many LLC services advertise low starting prices, but the final cost can change once you add registered agent service, operating agreement templates, EIN support, rush processing, or compliance tools. Always compare the full checkout price, not only the headline price.

2. Check state filing fees

Every state has its own LLC filing fee. Some states are inexpensive to file in, while others have higher initial or annual costs. State fees are usually charged separately from the service provider fee.

3. Decide whether you need a registered agent

A registered agent receives official legal and tax documents on behalf of your LLC. If you do not want to use your personal address or you are forming an LLC outside your home state, a professional registered agent service can be useful.

4. Think about ongoing compliance

Starting the LLC is only the first step. Many states require annual reports, franchise taxes, business licenses, or other filings. If you want reminders and support, Harbor Compliance may be a strong option. If you want a broader legal services platform, LegalZoom may be more convenient.

5. Consider future business needs

If you plan to hire employees, open bank accounts, apply for licenses, expand into another state, or raise capital, choose a service that can support more than the initial filing.

How to Start an LLC Online

The exact steps vary by state, but most LLC formations follow the same general process.

Step 1: Choose your state

Most small business owners form an LLC in the state where they live and operate. However, some entrepreneurs consider states such as Delaware, Wyoming, or Nevada for specific business reasons. If you form outside your home state, you may also need to register as a foreign LLC where you actually do business.

Step 2: Choose a business name

Your LLC name must usually be distinguishable from existing business names in the state. It also needs to include an LLC indicator such as “LLC” or “Limited Liability Company.”

Step 3: Appoint a registered agent

Your LLC needs a registered agent with a physical address in the state of formation. You can serve as your own agent in some cases, but many owners prefer a professional service for privacy and convenience.

Step 4: File Articles of Organization

The Articles of Organization officially create the LLC. An LLC service can prepare and submit this filing for you, or you can file directly with the state.

Step 5: Create an operating agreement

An operating agreement explains how the LLC is managed, how profits are handled, and what happens if ownership changes. Even single-member LLCs can benefit from having one.

Step 6: Get an EIN if needed

An Employer Identification Number is commonly needed for business banking, hiring employees, and certain tax filings. You can apply through the IRS, and many LLC services offer EIN assistance.

Step 7: Handle licenses and compliance

Depending on your business type and location, you may need local licenses, state permits, annual reports, or tax registrations. This is where compliance-focused support can become valuable.

Can I form an LLC myself?

Yes. You can usually file directly with the state. An LLC service is useful if you want help preparing documents, choosing add-ons, appointing a registered agent, or avoiding basic filing mistakes.

Do I need a registered agent?

Most states require an LLC to have a registered agent. You may be able to act as your own agent, but a professional registered agent service can help protect privacy and keep official notices organized.

Is LegalZoom worth it?

LegalZoom can be worth it if you want a well-known provider, a guided filing process, and access to broader legal and business services. It may not be the lowest-cost option for every user.

Is Harbor Compliance worth it?

Harbor Compliance can be worth it for businesses that value compliance support, registered agent service, and help with ongoing state requirements.

Is Swyft Filings worth it?

Swyft Filings can be worth it for users who want a simple LLC formation workflow and a fast way to get started online.

What is the cheapest way to start an LLC?

The cheapest way is often to file directly with the state and pay only the state filing fee. However, this means you handle the paperwork, registered agent decision, operating agreement, and compliance steps yourself.

What is the best state to form an LLC?

For most small business owners, the best state is the state where they live and operate. Wyoming and Delaware are popular for specific reasons, but forming out of state can create extra compliance requirements.

How long does it take to form an LLC?

Processing time depends on the state and whether you choose standard or expedited filing. Some states process filings quickly, while others may take longer.

Do I need an operating agreement?

An operating agreement is highly recommended. It explains ownership, management, profit distribution, and internal rules. It can be useful even for a single-member LLC.

Do I need an EIN for my LLC?

You may need an EIN for business banking, employees, tax filings, and certain business activities. Many LLC owners get one even when it is not immediately required.

Can a non-US resident start a US LLC?

In many cases, non-US residents can form a US LLC. However, tax, banking, registered agent, and compliance issues can be more complex, so professional guidance may be helpful.

Should I use one LLC service for multiple states?

If you plan to operate in multiple states, a compliance-focused provider may be useful. Harbor Compliance is especially relevant for businesses that need multi-state support.
